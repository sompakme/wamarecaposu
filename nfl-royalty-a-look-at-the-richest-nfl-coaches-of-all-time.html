NFL Royalty: A Look At The Richest NFL Coaches Of All Time

 Posted on September 18, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1416 words  |  Martina Birk

Highlights

  • Some NFL coaches have amassed significant fortunes throughout their careers, thanks to their strategic prowess and leadership skills.
  • Mike Holmgren, Mike Tomlin, and Steve Mariucci are among the richest NFL coaches of all time, with a net worth of $16 million each.
  • These coaches have achieved success through Super Bowl victories, enduring legacies, and leading their teams to the pinnacle of wealth in professional football.

The NFL has long been a breeding ground for exceptional talent – not only on the field, but also on the sidelines. While the players often take center stage, it's the coaches who play a pivotal role in shaping the destiny of their respective teams.

Beyond their strategic prowess and leadership skills, some NFL coaches have managed to amass vast fortunes throughout their careers. This article will delve into the world of the richest NFL coaches of all time, uncovering the financial success stories behind these gridiron legends.

From Super Bowl victories to enduring legacies, let’s examine the incredible journeys that have led these coaches to the pinnacle of wealth in the world of professional football.

10 Mike Holmgren: Net Worth $16 Million

Mike Holmgren hails from San Francisco, California, born in the year 1948. He kicked off his football journey as a quarterback for the University of Southern California (USC), securing a national championship during his collegiate tenure.

The St. Louis Cardinals selected him in the 1970 NFL Draft. However, his playing career didn't quite reach its potential, leading him to transition into coaching.

Subsequently, he took charge of coaching responsibilities at various college teams during the 1970s and 1980s. His foray into the NFL commenced when he assumed the role of quarterback coach for the San Francisco 49ers.

Over the course of his NFL coaching career, Holmgren achieved significant success, securing three NFC Championships and one Super Bowl victory.

9 Mike Tomlin: Net Worth $16 Million

Mike Tomlin currently holds the position of head coach for the Pittsburgh Steelers. He amassed his wealth while rising to prominence as one of the NFL's youngest coaches, and notably, he became the first black head coach in the history of the Steelers.

While Tomlin didn't have a professional playing career, he did have a background in high school football as a player. His coaching journey began as a wide receiver coach for Virginia Military Institute, and he subsequently coached various positions for college teams.

His entry into the NFL came as an assistant coach with the Tampa Bay Buccaneers. In 2007, he secured his first head coaching role with the Pittsburgh Steelers, leading them to a victorious Super Bowl XLIII.

8 Steve Mariucci: Net Worth $16 Million

Steve Mariucci hails from Iron Mountain, Michigan. He made his mark as a quarterback for Northern Michigan University, achieving the prestigious title of three-time All-American and even clinching the NCAA Division II National Football Championship.

Additionally, he briefly donned the jersey of the Canadian Football League's Hamilton Tiger-Cats. In 1978, Mariucci set off on his coaching odyssey, initially focusing on college teams.

His NFL journey commenced in 1992 when he assumed the role of quarterback coach for the Green Bay Packers, where he had the privilege of coaching the legendary Brett Favre. Subsequently, he ventured into head coaching with the San Francisco 49ers, guiding them to the NFC Championship game.

7 Gary Kubiak: Net Worth $18 Million

Gary Kubiak, a native of Houston, Texas, showcased his athletic prowess during his high school years, participating in football, basketball, track and field, and baseball. Following his graduation, he continued his football journey at Texas A&M, where he notched a conference record during his junior year.

In 1983, he was selected by the Denver Broncos in the NFL Draft and remained with the team until 1991, leaving an indelible mark. Kubiak's coaching career commenced in 1992 at Texas A&M, marking the beginning of his journey in football leadership.

He transitioned to the NFL as a quarterback coach for the San Francisco 49ers, achieving victory in Super Bowl XXIX.

6 Andy Reid: Net Worth $20 Million

Andy Reid, born in Los Angeles, California, pursued his football journey at BYU, where he played as an offensive guard. Following his graduation, he embarked on his coaching career, initially as an assistant coach and later as an offensive line coach for various schools.

In 1999, he took a significant step in his coaching journey by becoming the head coach of the Philadelphia Eagles. They clinched the Eastern Division title for four consecutive years from 2001 to 2004, and their journey culminated in winning the NFC Championship and securing a spot in Super Bowl XXXIX, though they narrowly missed the championship.

5 Pete Carroll: Net Worth $20 Million

Pete Carroll has left a significant mark as the head coach of the Seattle Seahawks, standing out as one of the few who successfully transitioned from college coaching to the NFL. He is renowned for his player-centric coaching style, employing positive reinforcement to unite his team towards a common goal.

The Seahawks have achieved six divisional-round playoff appearances and reached the Super Bowl in two consecutive years, clinching victory in their first attempt. Carroll initially spent fifteen years coaching in the NFL but eventually decided to return to the collegiate ranks.

During his tenure at USC, he transformed their football program into a formidable force.

4 Don Shula: Net Worth $30 Million

Don Shula's journey in the NFL began as a player for the Cleveland Browns in 1951, after which he moved to the Baltimore Colts and later the Washington Redskins. However, his true legacy would be forged as a coach; in 1963, he began on his coaching career as the head coach of the Baltimore Colts.

His coaching prowess carried over when he took the helm of the Miami Dolphins in 1970. It was during this time that he etched his name in NFL history by becoming the first coach to amass 100 wins in just ten seasons.

Under his guidance, the Dolphins achieved a flawless playoff run and a triumphant Super Bowl victory in the 1972-1973 season.

3 Mike Shanahan: Net Worth $30 Million

Mike Shanahan is from Oak Park, Illinois, and initially began his football career as a player. His journey is marked by a compelling backstory.

During his teenage years, he endured a tragic motorcycle accident that tragically took the life of his friend, while Mike miraculously survived. The following year, as he was participating in football activities during his junior year at spring football practice for Eastern Illinois, he encountered a life-threatening collision on the field.

Driven by an enduring love for football, he turned his attention to coaching. Embracing this new path, he took on various coaching roles within the college football arena.

2 Bill Belichick: Net Worth $35 Million

Bill Belichick is undeniably one of the most accomplished coaches in NFL history. He also ranks as the second-wealthiest NFL coach of all time, boasting a net worth of $35 million.

His coaching journey got its start in 1975 with the Baltimore Colts. He then ascended to the role of defensive coordinator for the New York Giants, before assuming the head coaching position for the Cleveland Browns.

However, it was in 2000 that Belichick made a pivotal move that would define his legacy. He took the helm as the head coach of the New England Patriots, and under his guidance, the team achieved unprecedented success.

Bill Belichick has led his team to numerous Super Bowl appearances, securing a remarkable 5 victories.

1 Jimmy Johnson: Net Worth $45 Million

Jimmy Johnson holds the enviable distinction of being the wealthiest coach in NFL history, boasting a current net worth of $45 million. While he has amassed his fortune through coaching, he has also left an enduring legacy in the world of football.

His coaching journey began way back in 1965, when he took on the role of assistant coach at Louisiana Tech University. Over the years, he assumed coaching positions at various colleges, undertaking the formidable task of revitalizing the University of Miami team and reversing their streak of losses.

Under his guidance, the team, which had previously experienced more losses than wins, enjoyed an undefeated season.
