Michael Douglas Thanks Zeta-Jones, Makes Gay Sex Jokes at Emmys

 Posted on July 5, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  302 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Michael Douglas was all about the innuendo at the 2013 Emmy Awards in L.A. Winning his Best Actor in a TV-Movie or Miniseries for his role in Behind the Candelabra, the 68-actor was in a jubilant, playful mood. “Thank you my academy, fellow members, thank you so much and to the great performances by all the other nominees,” Douglas began, acknowledging his competitors Toby Jones, Benedict Cumberbatch and Al Pacino. “But obviously there’s one nominee in particular that I have to really thank: Matt.”

That would be Matt Damon, his costar in the acclaimed HBO Liberace biopic, in which Douglas played the beloved pianist opposite Damon, taking on the role of Liberace’s lover, Scott Thorson. “This is a two-hander, and you’re only as good as your other hand,” said Douglas as the camera panned to Damon — who instantly blushed and giggled. (Damon and Douglas didn’t flinch filming frank scenes of gay sex in the film.)

“You were magnificent,” Douglas raved to a smiling Damon, 40. “And the only reason i’m standing her is because you. so you really deserve half of this. So, you want the bottom or the top?” he added to more audience titters.
