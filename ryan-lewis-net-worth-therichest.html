Currently based in Seattle, Ryan Lewis is an American disc jockey, musician and producer with an estimated net worth of $18 million.

Ryan Lewis was born on March 25, 1988 in Spokane, Washington, USA. Along with Macklemore, Lewis received the Best Group award at the 2013 BET Awards. On the same year, his single "Thrift Shop" won the Rap Song of the Year award at the 2013 Billboard Music Awards.

In the past, he has produced several albums, which include The Heist, The VS. EP and Instrumentals. He also directed, engineered, recorded and produced several albums in collaboration with his friend Macklemore. Among the albums are Can't Hold Us, White Walls, Otherside, And We Danced, Thrift Shop, and Same Love.

Released in October 2012, The Heist debuted at #2 on Billboard charts. The album peaked at #1 on Top Rap Albums and Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums. Within a week of its release, the album had sold 78,000 copies. On Soundcloud, it earned more than a million plays, while on YouTube, it received more than 350 million views.
