The Most Expensive Homes Owned By Footballers

 Posted on August 5, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1503 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Highlights

  • Football stars like Beckham, Ronaldo, and Messi have earned massive amounts of money and can afford some of the most expensive houses in the world.
  • These extravagant homes with luxurious features reflect the personal prosperity and global appeal of modern football.
  • From home theaters and swimming pools to private football pitches, these lavish homes showcase the wealth and success of the players who own them.

Football has changed a lot over the years, evolving from a common game played for fun, to a massive source of global entertainment and revenue.

Many players have become famous through football, like Cristiano Ronaldo, Lionel Messi, and David Beckham (to name a few). They make so much money that they can afford some of the most expensive houses in the world.

These houses provide fans with a peek into the fabulous lives of these celebrity players, and mansions are seen as symbols of a player's ability and success. Adorned with luxurious features and situated in prime locations, these extravagant dwellings epitomize personal prosperity while underscoring the vast global appeal and financial power of modern football.

12 Sergio Aguero

£2.8 Million

The talented striker, who graced Manchester City's ranks along with his national team Argentina, resides in an elegant house nestled within Hale Barns. In 2014, Sergio Aguero acquired this contemporary property for £2.8 million.

This amazing house has six big bedrooms, five bathrooms, a home theater, a bar that's great for entertaining guests, a soothing Jacuzzi to relax in after games or practices, and a big garage for all his cars. Additionally, Aguero possesses properties located in Buenos Aires and Madrid, from his earlier stint at Atlético Madrid (before joining Manchester City).

11 Paul Pogba

£3.5 Million

The stylish French midfielder (representing Juventus in Series A alongside France) has found solace within an exquisite residence based in Cheshire. Purchased for £3.5 million three years ago, this lavish mansion holds grandeur beyond compare.

This house has everything - five spacious bedrooms to sleep in, four bathrooms, a heated indoor pool to swim in, a high-tech home gym where Pogba can stay fit, a game room packed with fun, a media lounge to enjoy films, and a balcony for some quiet time with nature.

A grand foyer leads into this palatial paradise, while vaulted ceilings reach for the sky. Beyond this magnificent home Pogba owns several high-end vehicles, including a Rolls-Royce, a Lamborghini, and a Ferrari.

10 Didier Drogba

£3.8 million

Among the most influential forwards of his time, this Ivorian legend achieved remarkable success with Chelsea FC and represented Ivory Coast on numerous occasions.

Didier Drogba currently resides in an extraordinary mansion situated in Surrey, purchased for £3.8 million back in 2010. Exuding opulence, this majestic home offers seven generously sized bedrooms, eight bathrooms – also housing a sports museum that serves as a testament to Drogba's accomplishments, further adorned with several smaller rooms dedicated to children.

9 Gareth Bale

£4 million

Famous for his blazing speed and exceptional skills as a winger, Gareth Bale remains one of the finest players worldwide. His residence, located in Madrid, is among the top ten priciest homes owned by footballers.

Offering contemporary architecture, the property was purchased for £4 million during his transfer to Real Madrid in 2013. Comprising four elegant bedrooms, a capacious six-car garage providing ample space, a refreshing pool, and an indoor gymnasium, Bale's house is one of the most expensive homes in football.

8 Andres Iniesta

£4.1 million

One of the best midfielders of this generation, Andres Iniesta accrued numerous awards during exemplary spells at Barcelona FC and while representing Spain. Located in the splendorous Castelldefels region, Iniesta's property is worth around £4.1 million, and was constructed roughly a decade ago.

Serving as both sanctuary and symbol, his name alongside shirt number embellishes its exterior wall, demonstrating allegiance towards both Barcelona FC and Spain. Featuring five serene bedrooms, a sparkling swimming pool, and even boasting a small contemplative chapel, this magnificent home epitomizes elegance.

7 John Terry

£5 million

Esteemed for his stellar defensive capabilities, captaining Chelsea FC along with England to glory over illustrious careers, veteran defender John Terry occupies a breathtaking domicile within Surrey.

Acquired for £5 million in 2018, this splendid residence consists of nine regal bedrooms and eight luxurious bathrooms, providing abundant space to unwind. A cinema room, wine cellar, gym, sauna, swimming pool, and an outdoor kitchen ensure that Terry's home is the epitome of luxury and comfort.

6 Nicky Butt

£5.5 million

Nicky Butt – a member of the famous "Class of '92," which achieved multiple victories with Manchester United – owns an expensive mansion in Hale Barns.

The house (purchased for £5.5 million in 2014) includes six bedrooms and bathrooms, as well as luxurious amenities such as a cinema room, wine cellar, swimming pool, spa facilities, and a football pitch.

5 Lionel Messi

£5.7 million

Lionel Messi, Ronaldo's prominent competitor and holder of seven Ballon d'Or awards, resides in an innovative residence located in Barcelona. Acquired for £5.7 million in 2009, the property resembles a football shape and comprises four bedrooms and bathrooms, along with a scenic view of the Mediterranean Sea.

Additional features include a swimming pool, a gymnasium area, and a private cinema room. It is noteworthy to mention that Messi owns other properties across Argentina and Spain.

4 Cristiano Ronaldo

£8 million

Another "priciest residence" belongs to famous footballer Cristiano Ronaldo. Widely considered one of the greatest athletes in history and having achieved five Ballon d'Or accolades, this Portuguese icon currently resides in Saudi Arabia.

Upon his transfer to Juventus back in 2018, he acquired a property for approximately £8 million. This extravagant home boasts four bedrooms, five bathrooms, an indoor pool, a well-equipped gymnasium (alongside a sauna), and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the vibrant cityscape.

Additionally, Ronaldo's notable real estate portfolio includes several luxurious dwellings spread across the globe – an apartment located in Lisbon, a penthouse dwelling high above picturesque New York City, and even an exceptional private island in Madeira.

3 Kaka

£9 million

One of the priciest residences owned by a footballer belongs to Kaka, widely regarded as one of the finest midfielders in recent times. This Brazilian icon resides in an exquisite villa in Madrid, which he acquired upon joining Real Madrid back in 2009.

Boasting seven bedrooms, eight bathrooms, and an array of luxurious amenities like a swimming pool, tennis court, gymnasium, and cinema room, this property is truly remarkable. Apart from his Madrid abode, Kaka owns other real estate holdings both in Brazil and Orlando.

2 Wayne Rooney

£20 million

Wayne Rooney, a famous record-breaking scorer for both England and Manchester United, owns one of the priciest home among footballers. Nestled in Cheshire, this esteemed dwelling was meticulously crafted by Wayne and his wife Coleen to manifest their ultimate aspirations.

Rooney's house is worth $20 million, with six bedrooms, eight bathrooms, a movie theater, a wine cellar, and a game room with a pool table. There's also a swimming pool, a spa, and a gym to stay fit – along with a private football pitch to play soccer.

Complementing their prestigious primary property is another sanctuary nestled on the picturesque shores of Barbados, acquired for £5 million.

1 David Beckham

£39.5 million

Finally, the owner of the most valuable residence among professional football players happens to be David Beckham, known for his opulent home located in the cost prohibitive City of London.

This lavish property was acquired by the former captain of England's national team and his wife Victoria back in 2014, at a staggering cost of £31.5 million. Moreover, the Beckhams invested approximately £8 million on renovations to further enhance its grandeur.

Their property is located in the popular Holland Park area, and has a ton of luxurious features. There are six large bedrooms, eight expertly designed and decorated bathrooms, a full gym for workouts, an extensive wine cellar, a private theater for watching movies, a beauty salon, and an underground garage to park in privacy.

The earning potential present in soccer is evident in the homes of football stars like Beckham, Ronaldo and Messi. Their homes have perks like home gyms, wine cellars, and sports complexes, illustrating how much money they have earned and the reach of their stardom.

Ranging from expansive estates to culturally significant homes, these properties underscore how top-tier footballers shape their personal brands. Each residence possesses its own unique value and location, highlighting just how exclusive modern football can be for its most accomplished protagonists.
