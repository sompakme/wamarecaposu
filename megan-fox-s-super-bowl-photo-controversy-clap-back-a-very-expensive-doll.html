Highlights

  • A Megan Fox Super Bowl photo spread virally, with media outlets criticizing the candid snap.
  • Fox addressed social media comments about her appearance, dismissing claims that she looked "unrecognizable" in a Super Bowl photo.
  • The controversy surrounding female celebrities being labeled "unrecognizable" after high-profile events is common in Hollywood.
  • Fox responded to the controversy on Instagram, poking fun at the speculation and emphasizing that she embraces her unique appearance.

Megan Fox has responded to social media gossipmongers after an after party photograph of the actress went viral in early February.

The controversy began when a Megan Fox Super Bowl photo began circulating, captured during an after-party for Super Bowl LVIII.

As is often the case with women in Hollywood, entertainment news sites and social media users claimed Fox was "unrecognizable."

Claims that a female celebrity has become "unrecognizable" are routinely levied after images from high-profile events like the Super Bowl or red carpet galas are published.

Uma Thurman and Renée Zellweger are among stars slapped with the label — which appears to be a euphemistic allegation, insinuating a celebrity has "had work done," i.e., has undergone plastic surgery or other cosmetic interventions.

The Megan Fox Super Bowl Photo

On February 14th, InTouch Weekly covered the Megan Fox Super Bowl photo, describing Fox as "unrecognizable," and attributing the observation to "fans":

"The Jennifer’s Body star, 37, posed for a photo with fiancé Machine Gun Kelly, Travis Kelce and Taylor Swift at the celebratory bash on Sunday, February 11, after the Kansas City Chiefs beat the San Francisco 49ers. Fans were quick to point out the changes in Megan’s appearance."

As indicated, Fox was photographed with her partner (Machine Gun Kelly), Travis Kelce, and Taylor Swift.

Of the viral photo of Fox, the outlet added:

"Megan’s signature dark brown locks were dyed a pinkish blonde and pulled into a bun on the crown of her head, with face framing pieces left out at the front. Meanwhile, she sported dark eye makeup and bright red lips that looked plump as she made a duck face and held up a peace sign.

"The actress went for a casual gothic look in a black sweater featuring a skeletal hand and a spiked choker around her neck."

Also on the 14th, tabloid The Mirror described Fox's Super Bowl photo as one in which she was "unrecognizable," going so far as to consult an "aesthetics practitioner" for analysis:

"The 37-year-old actress seemed to be having a great time as she posed for a picture with her fiancé Machine Gun Kelly and their pals Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce.

"Some fans were distracted by Megan's appearance and many suggested she has undergone more plastic surgery.

"Aesthetics Practitioner Amish Patel believes this could be the case, but also believes Megan's changing appearance could simply be down to ageing. He told The Mirror: 'It looks like Megan may have had some lip filler.'"

Megan Fox's Super Bowl Photo Clapback

On February 14th, Fox took to Instagram to address the controversy:

In the post's caption, Fox directly addressed social media commentary about her appearance.

Fox specifically spoke to claims she looked like a "doll," writing:

"oh my god guys look how different i … dont look at all. turns out it was just a shadowy cell phone pic of me looking like a ukrainian blowup doll.

when in REALITY i look like one of those super expensive silicone real [adult] dolls you can only get in japan 💁🏻‍♀️"

Top comments on Fox's post largely critiqued her mentions of Ukraine and Japan, but commenters didn't criticize her appearance much, if at all.

While the Swifties on r/TaylorSwift were reasonably polite in their analysis of the Megan Fox Super Bowl photo, that was not the case for posters on the gossip-centric subreddit r/FauxMoi.

In the early morning hours of February 15th, a post was shared to r/FauxMoi, critiquing Fox's Instagram response:

Again, several Reddit users compared Fox to Kim Kardashian, commenting:

"unrelated but anyone else think she was kim k in the second pic?"

"Uh yes!! She’s literally morphing into a clone of them[.]"

"They're all morphing into each other."

Notably, those observations were not specific to Megan Fox, nor the Super Bowl photo controversy.

Claims that "all girls on Instagram look the same" or that they "have Instagram face" have been popular for several years, mainly criticism of the homogenous "look" popular on the platform.

Finally, the Megan Fox Super Bowl photo controversy (and a secondary controversy over her response) were not the first time Fox has grappled with intense scrutiny of her appearance in the press or on social media.

People covered Fox's Instagram response, noting that she has said she struggles with body dysmorphia, and she once told the outlet there was "never a point in [her] life" where she felt comfortable with her appearance.
