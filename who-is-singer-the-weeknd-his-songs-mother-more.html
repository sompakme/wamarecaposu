Biography Timeline

Abel Makkonen Tesfaye is a Grammy-winning Canadian singer, songwriter, and record producer. He first gained widespread recognition in 2010 with a series of mixtapes and has since become one of the biggest names in contemporary R&B and pop music.

1990: Born on February 16 in Toronto, Ontario, Canada.

2010: Began releasing mixtapes anonymously under the moniker “The Weeknd.”

2011: Released his first mixtape, “House of Balloons,” which received widespread critical acclaim.

2012: Released his first studio album, “Trilogy,” which combined remastered versions of his first three mixtapes.

2013: Became a household name with the release of his album “Kiss Land,” which included the hit single “Wicked Games.”

2015: Released his second studio album, “Beauty Behind the Madness,” which included the hit single “Can’t Feel My Face.”

2016: Won two Grammy Awards for his work on the soundtrack for the film “Fifty Shades of Grey.”

2018: Released his fourth studio album, “My Dear Melancholy,” which departed from his previous pop-oriented sound and showcased a more experimental and reflective style.

2020: Released his fifth studio album, “After Hours,” which returned to his signature pop-infused R&B sound.

Throughout his career has established himself as one of his generation’s most innovative and influential musicians, consistently pushing the boundaries of contemporary music with his genre-defying sound and experimental approach to production and songwriting.
